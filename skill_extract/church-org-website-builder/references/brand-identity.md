# Brand Identity Reference — Church & Organization Website Builder

## Aesthetic Directions (Assign ONE Per Organization)

Choose the direction that best matches the organization's signals — theology, scale, tone, audience, and existing visual identity. When signals are mixed, the theological emphasis is the tiebreaker.

---

### 1. Sacred Luxury Editorial
**Feels like:** A cathedral at midnight. High-fashion editorial meets prophetic assembly space.
**When to use:** Word-based churches, evangelical ministries, prophetic organizations, formal pulpit culture, high-doctrine teaching emphasis
**Visual hallmarks:** Near-black backgrounds with warm or purple tint, gold or warm amber accents, classical serif typography, editorial whitespace, gold gradient decorative rules, film grain texture
**Dark palette base:** bg #07040E–#120828, accent #B8920A–#E8C47A, text #F0E8D5
**Light palette base:** bg #FAFAF8–#F0EBF8, text #1A0D35
**Inspiration:** Vatican gold + Vogue editorial darkness + Criterion Collection covers
**Default for:** Any church whose brand language includes "reign", "word of God", "covenant", "creative word"

---

### 2. Power & Presence
**Feels like:** Stadium worship. Epic scale. Overwhelming divine authority.
**When to use:** Large-scale churches, megachurch, dominion theology ministries, mass-attendance worship, gospel choirs, broadcast-first ministries
**Visual hallmarks:** Deep purple/midnight blue with crimson and gold, bold heavy type, dramatic god-ray lighting effects, strong horizontal rules, full-bleed photography
**Dark palette base:** bg #0C0620, accent #D4AF37 or #9B1D42, text #EEE8E0
**Light palette base:** bg #F8F5FF, text #1A0830
**Inspiration:** Elevation Worship identity + Royal Philharmonic program + IMAX title sequence
**Default for:** Any church whose language includes "authority", "dominion", "anointing", "global impact"

---

### 3. Afro-Spiritual Warmth
**Feels like:** Sacred Africa. Community fire. Kente cloth woven into stained glass.
**When to use:** African praise & worship ministries, community-centered churches, pan-African organizations, culturally rooted missions, African traditional + Christian fusion
**Visual hallmarks:** Rich earth tones (ochre, mahogany, terracotta, ivory), geometric pattern accents, warm serif typography, cultural pride elements, warm amber/copper accent
**Dark palette base:** bg #1A0F0A–#2C1A0F, accent #D4891A or #C4622D, text #F7F0E3
**Light palette base:** bg #FDF5EC, text #1A0F0A
**Inspiration:** Angelique Kidjo visual identity + Kenyan textile patterns + Ethiopian Orthodox iconography
**Default for:** Any church emphasizing African identity, community celebration, and cultural roots

---

### 4. Contemporary Light
**Feels like:** A modern gathering space on a Sunday morning. Bright, accessible, welcoming.
**When to use:** Young congregation churches, modern evangelical, seeker-friendly, casual ministry culture, social-media-native organizations
**Visual hallmarks:** Clean white or off-white backgrounds, vibrant single accent (electric blue, deep teal, warm orange), bold sans-serif type for labels, editorial photography, large open whitespace
**Dark palette base:** bg #0A0F1C–#131C32, accent #2563EB or #059669, text #F8FAFC
**Light palette base:** bg #FAFAFA–#F1F5F9, text #0F172A
**Inspiration:** Life.Church + Hillsong's clean branding + Apple Store aesthetic adapted for ministry
**Default for:** Any church whose language is casual, relational, uses "community", "family", "journey"

---

### 5. Raw & Reverent
**Feels like:** An underground church. Acoustic warmth. Vulnerability and intimacy.
**When to use:** Intimate congregations, grassroots ministries, acoustic/folk worship, house church movements, small-group focused organizations
**Visual hallmarks:** Warm earthy tones (terracotta, burnt sienna, warm cream, charcoal), hand-crafted feel, grain overlays, natural textures, candlelit warmth, imperfect edges
**Dark palette base:** bg #1C1814–#2A2118, accent #C4622D or #D4891A, text #F5EFE6
**Light palette base:** bg #FAF7F2–#F0E8DC, text #1C1814
**Inspiration:** Maverick City Music warmth + hand-lettered zine aesthetic + film-photography warmth
**Default for:** Any church emphasizing authenticity, vulnerability, confession, and intimate community

## Color System Rules

### The 8-Color Palette (Dark Theme)

Every brand palette has exactly 8 named roles:

```
1. PRIMARY_BG    — Darkest background. The base of the entire site. Must have a slight
                   color tint (warm/cool/purple) — never pure black #000000.
                   Range: #050310 – #1C1410 depending on aesthetic direction.

2. SECONDARY_BG  — Panel and section backgrounds. Visibly distinct from PRIMARY_BG
                   when viewed side-by-side in a dark room. 1-2 Lightness steps lighter.

3. TERTIARY_BG   — Card, component, and input backgrounds. Has a surface feel.
                   Used for: sermon cards, ministry tiles, form backgrounds, pullquote bg.

4. ACCENT        — The brand's defining color. This is the organization's signature tone.
                   Derived directly from their existing branding or aesthetic direction.
                   Used sparingly: CTAs, section labels, decorative rules, stats numbers.
                   Must cover less than 15% of any screen.

5. ACCENT_LIGHT  — 35% lighter tint of ACCENT. Used for: Display font italics,
                   pull quote text, hover state backgrounds, number counters.

6. ACCENT_DARK   — 30% darker shade of ACCENT. Used for: borders, subtle underlines,
                   background accents, hover borders. Never as readable text on dark bg.

7. TEXT_PRIMARY  — Main readable text. Cream or warm off-white (never pure #FFFFFF).
                   Range: #EEE8E0 – #F5F0EA. Must pass WCAG AA on PRIMARY_BG.

8. TEXT_MUTED    — Supporting text. Around 55-65% luminance of TEXT_PRIMARY.
                   Used for: metadata, captions, secondary labels, paragraph lead text.
```

### Light Theme (5 Additional Colors)

```
PRIMARY_BG_LT    — Clean near-white with slight tint (never pure #FFFFFF)
SECONDARY_BG_LT  — Pale tinted panel background
TERTIARY_BG_LT   — Light card/component surface
TEXT_PRIMARY_LT  — Deep, dark version of brand identity (often the dark bg color)
TEXT_MUTED_LT    — Mid-tone muted for supporting text on light backgrounds
(ACCENT, ACCENT_LIGHT, ACCENT_DARK remain the same in both themes)
```

### Evocative Color Naming Convention

**NEVER use generic names:**
❌ "Dark Blue", "Light Gold", "Medium Grey", "Off-White"

**ALWAYS use evocative names from the organization's world:**
✅ Draw from: faith language, African geography, liturgical objects, time of day, ritual, nature, scripture imagery

**Name banks by accent type:**

Gold/Amber accent: "Covenant Gold", "Prophet Gold", "Altar Flame", "Glory Light", "Wisdom Ember", "Ancient Amber"
Purple accent: "Throne Room Purple", "Sacred Canopy", "Royal Night", "Anointing Oil", "Incense Mist"
Crimson/Red: "Prophetic Fire", "Blood Covenant", "Redemption Crimson", "Pentecost Flame"
Teal/Green: "Still Waters", "Promised Land", "Healing Spring", "Shepherd's Green"
Earth tones: "Ochre Earth", "African Clay", "Mahogany Deep", "Harvest Gold", "Terracotta Fire"
Background darks: "Throne Room Black", "Sanctuary Night", "Midnight Assembly", "Deep Covenant", "Sacred Dark"
Text tones: "Parchment", "Scripture Cream", "Linen Light", "Dawn White", "Holy Vellum"
Muted tones: "Incense Mist", "Desert Dust", "River Silt", "Cloud Shadow", "Veil Grey"

### CSS Custom Properties Pattern

```css
/* === DARK THEME === */
:root, [data-theme="dark"] {
  --bg:        [PRIMARY_BG];
  --panel:     [SECONDARY_BG];
  --card:      [TERTIARY_BG];
  --accent:    [ACCENT];
  --accent-lt: [ACCENT_LIGHT];
  --accent-dk: [ACCENT_DARK];
  --text:      [TEXT_PRIMARY];
  --muted:     [TEXT_MUTED];
}

/* === LIGHT THEME === */
[data-theme="light"] {
  --bg:        [PRIMARY_BG_LT];
  --panel:     [SECONDARY_BG_LT];
  --card:      [TERTIARY_BG_LT];
  --accent:    [ACCENT];          /* Same as dark */
  --accent-lt: [ACCENT_LIGHT];   /* Same as dark */
  --accent-dk: [ACCENT_DARK];    /* Same as dark */
  --text:      [TEXT_PRIMARY_LT];
  --muted:     [TEXT_MUTED_LT];
}
```

### Contrast Requirements
- TEXT_PRIMARY on PRIMARY_BG: ≥ 4.5:1 (WCAG AA)
- ACCENT on PRIMARY_BG: ≥ 3:1 for large text (CTAs and headlines)
- ACCENT_LIGHT on SECONDARY_BG: verify visually before finalizing
- Never use ACCENT_DARK as text color on dark backgrounds

## Typography System

### The Trio Rule — Exactly 3 Typefaces

**DISPLAY** — Used for: hero headlines, section headings, pull quotes, Display italic carries emotional weight
```
Options (ordered by recommendation):
  Cormorant Garamond — Best for: Sacred Luxury, Power & Presence, Raw & Reverent
  Playfair Display   — Best for: Contemporary Light, any modern ministry
  DM Serif Display   — Best for: Contemporary Light, young congregation
  Spectral           — Best for: academic/word-study emphasis
  Libre Caslon       — Best for: traditional evangelical
```

**CAPS** — Used for: navigation links, section tags, labels, CTA button text — ALWAYS UPPERCASE, always letter-spaced ≥0.12em
```
Options:
  Cinzel            — Best for: Sacred Luxury, Power & Presence, royal/formal
  Marcellus SC      — Best for: Afro-Spiritual, warm traditional
  Josefin Sans      — Best for: Contemporary Light, modern/young
  Tenor Sans        — Best for: Contemporary Light, accessible
  Cinzel Decorative — For decorative use only (not navigation — too heavy)
```

**BODY** — Used for: paragraphs, bios, ministry descriptions, form labels — never uppercase, never letter-spaced
```
Options:
  Crimson Pro       — Best for: Sacred Luxury, editorial warmth
  Lora              — Best for: Power & Presence, academic feel
  Merriweather      — Best for: Raw & Reverent, Afro-Spiritual warmth
  EB Garamond       — Best for: formal/traditional evangelical
  Libre Baskerville — Best for: strong academic/word-study emphasis
```

### Type Scale

```
Display headline:  clamp(3.5rem, 8vw, 8rem)    — Hero H1 only
Section heading:   clamp(2rem, 4vw, 3.5rem)     — H2 section titles
Sub-heading:       clamp(1.3rem, 2.5vw, 2rem)   — H3 within sections
Caps label:        0.52rem, tracking 0.22em      — Always CAPS font, always uppercase
Body text:         1rem (16px minimum always)
Caption / meta:    0.8rem                        — Dates, speaker names, categories
Mono (code):       0.76rem                       — In enhancement guide only
```

### Type Rules — Non-Negotiable
- CAPS font is ONLY used uppercase, letter-spacing ≥0.12em always
- Display font in italic signals emphasis, emotion, or sacred weight
- Body font never uppercase, never letter-spaced
- Font roles are non-interchangeable — never mix
- Line-height: 0.9–1.1 for Display, 1.2–1.4 for headings, 1.65–1.8 for body
- Font sizes MUST use clamp() for display/heading — never fixed rem for responsive text
- Body text minimum 16px on mobile — never smaller

## Logotype System

### Organizational Logotype (Not Personal Name)

For a church/org, the logotype typically has 3 lines:

```
LINE 1: Primary identifier word(s)   — CAPS font, weight 400–500, tracking 0.25–0.35em, TEXT_PRIMARY
LINE 2: Subordinate identifier       — Display font, italic, weight 300–400, ACCENT_LIGHT
LINE 3: Descriptor                   — CAPS font, 0.45–0.5rem, tracking 0.35em, TEXT_MUTED

Separated by gradient rules:
background: linear-gradient(90deg, transparent, ACCENT, transparent); height: 1px;

EXAMPLE:
  ROYAL               ← Cinzel, 500, tracking 0.30em, cream
  ────── gold rule ──────
  Assembly            ← Cormorant Garamond italic, 300, gold-light
  CHURCH · NAIROBI    ← Cinzel, 0.45rem, tracking 0.35em, muted
```

### Monogram Mark

2–3 letters (typically acronym of the org — "RA" for Royal Assembly, "HC" for Harvest Chapel).
- Font: Display (e.g., Cormorant Garamond), weight 700, italic
- Color: ACCENT_LIGHT
- Container: Thin ACCENT border square (2px, rounded 2px) or circle
- Uses: favicon (32px), loading screen (80px), watermarks (200px+, opacity 0.02)

### Light Background Version

- Line 1 (CAPS): TEXT_PRIMARY_LT (deep dark color)
- Line 2 (Display italic): ACCENT
- Gradient rule: same but may need to adjust opacity

## Brand Voice Framework

### 4+1 Keywords

Format: `[Word] · [Word] · [Word] · [Word] · (not: [Word])`

Each keyword needs a 1-sentence explanation of what it means for this specific organization.

**Framework for derivation:**

1. **Theological keyword** — From their core teaching emphasis (e.g., Sovereign, Prophetic, Transformative, Restorative)
2. **Mission keyword** — From their stated vision/mission (e.g., Word-Built, Community-First, Marketplace-Focused, Youth-Driven)
3. **Tone keyword** — From their content style (e.g., Intimate, Bold, Grounded, Academic)
4. **Cultural keyword** — From their geographic/cultural identity (e.g., Rooted, Pan-African, Nairobi-Born, Global)
5. **Anti-keyword** — What they are NOT (e.g., not: Religious, not: Performative, not: Entertainment-First, not: Academic)

### Copy Patterns by Section

| Section | Tone | Pattern |
|---------|------|---------|
| Hero headline | Declarative, maximum weight | "Where Lives Are [Verb]." or "[Noun]. [Noun]. [Noun]." |
| Hero sub-heading | Single sentence, ministry-focused | "[Tagline from official source]" |
| Hero CTA Primary | Active verb | "JOIN LIVE SERVICE", "WATCH SERMONS", "LISTEN NOW" |
| Hero CTA Secondary | Discoverable | "LEARN MORE →", "OUR STORY →", "EXPLORE SERMONS" |
| Stats bar | Factual + evocative label | "20+ · YEARS OF MINISTRY", "6 · MINISTRY ARMS" |
| Section eyebrow | CAPS, context-setting | "OUR MINISTRIES · [ORG ABBREVIATION]" |
| Pull quote | Direct, scriptural, first person or pastoral | Exact quote from leader or scripture |
| CTA buttons | Short active verbs | Register, Join, Watch, Give, Connect, Partner |
| Newsletter | Pastoral invitation | "Receive the Word Directly" or "Stay Connected to the Ministry" |
| Footer note | Grounded, purposeful | "[Tagline]. [City], [Country] [Flag]" |
| Footer copyright | Legal, minimal | "© [Year] [Org Name] / [Abbreviation]. All rights reserved." |

## Aesthetic Execution Rules

### Darkness Discipline
- PRIMARY_BG must have visible color tint (not pure black) — the tint reflects the ministry's spiritual identity
- SECONDARY_BG must be perceptibly lighter than PRIMARY_BG in a dark room
- TERTIARY_BG (cards) must feel like a distinct surface, not a void

### Accent Usage Rule — 15% Maximum
The accent color touches no more than 15% of any screen. Use for:
- CTA buttons (primary only)
- Section eyebrow labels (CAPS font)
- Decorative gradient rules (1px horizontal lines)
- Pull quote marks (giant, near-invisible, 5-8% opacity)
- Stats counter numbers
- Hover border states
- Active nav indicator

Never use accent for: paragraph text, large fills, multiple adjacent elements

### Film Grain Texture (Always Apply)
Add subtle cinematic depth to every site:
```css
body::before {
  content: '';
  position: fixed; inset: 0;
  background-image: url("data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg viewBox='0 0 256 256' xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%3E%3Cfilter id='n'%3E%3CfeTurbulence type='fractalNoise' baseFrequency='0.9' numOctaves='4' stitchTiles='stitch'/%3E%3C/filter%3E%3Crect width='100%25' height='100%25' filter='url(%23n)' opacity='1'/%3E%3C/svg%3E");
  opacity: 0.03;        /* Range: 0.025 – 0.04 */
  pointer-events: none;
  z-index: 9998;
}
```

### Watermark Background Text
In large hero or section backgrounds, place the org's name, abbreviation, or a key word as a massive (8–14rem), near-invisible (opacity: 0.015–0.025) italic text. Creates depth without visual weight.

### Horizontal Rules
Always gradient-faded at edges — never solid:
```css
background: linear-gradient(90deg, transparent, var(--accent), transparent);
height: 1px;
```

### Photography Treatment
- Real leadership/pastoral photos must be provided by the client — never substitute
- If not provided: use org monogram placeholder in brand palette (no external person's photo)
- Atmospheric backgrounds: Unsplash images of crowds, light, landscape — OK for backgrounds
- All real photos: treat with subtle gold/brand-color overlay (opacity 0.03–0.06)
- Mark clearly in PRD: ⚠️ REAL PHOTO REQUIRED — [describe what's needed]

### Dark/Light Theme Toggle
- Default: dark (always)
- Light is user-toggled
- Theme stored in localStorage via next-themes
- Respect prefers-color-scheme on first visit
- All CSS custom properties transition: 0.4s ease
- Toggle button: sun/moon icon in nav, animated rotation on switch
